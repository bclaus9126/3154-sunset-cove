Sean - a hundred and seventy-nine days is a long time to wait. Whether you had somewhere you needed to be or just wanted this chapter behind you, six months on the market without an offer takes a real toll. I looked at your listing the way a buyer's agent would, and I think the story is actually pretty clear. Your home is not the problem.

Below are the four things I think held it back - specifically, and with no pitch attached. Each one is fixable.

Brad Claus
JLA Realty
1The price was set for a market that has moved on
When this home first listed in April 2023 at $374,000, the New Braunfels market was still near its peak. Since then it has corrected. The county tax authority assessed this property at $332,500 for 2025 - a 10.7 percent drop from the prior year. At $369,900, the listing was sitting above the range where active buyers in this neighborhood are actually transacting. Buyers do the math quickly, and when they find a better value a few streets over, they move on. Repricing to $329,900 - inside the real range, at a clean number - is the single move that changes the equation.
2A second expiration changes what buyers assume
This is the second time this home has expired. The first listing ran from April to September 2023, with a small reduction before being removed. This listing ran 179 more days at nearly the same price. Any buyer who has been actively searching New Braunfels over the past two to three years has already seen this address - and passed on it - twice. At that point it is not about the home. It is about what the history signals. Buyers assume something was not disclosed, even when nothing is wrong. A fresh MLS number, new photos, and a real launch day is the only way to reset that perception completely.
3An empty home is a hard home to fall in love with
Every interior photo in your listing shows a fully vacant home. At this price point, buyers are comparing your listing side by side with homes that are occupied or professionally staged. Empty rooms photographed with wide-angle lenses feel cold on screen - buyers cannot gauge how their furniture will fit, and they cannot feel the warmth of the space. The living room and primary bedroom are where buyers make the emotional decision to schedule a showing. Both were empty. Even light staging in those two rooms transforms how a buyer connects with the home on their first scroll.
4No virtual tour filtered out a key part of the buyer pool
Your listing had no virtual tour or video walkthrough. New Braunfels draws a meaningful share of buyers from outside the immediate area - people relocating from Austin, Dallas, or out of state who pre-screen listings online before committing to a showing. A Matterport or video walkthrough is how those buyers stay engaged long enough to put a home on their shortlist. Without it, a 179-day listing competing for attention against more media-rich options was working at a disadvantage it did not need to have.
